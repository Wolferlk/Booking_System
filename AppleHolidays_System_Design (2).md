# AppleHolidays MMT_Vietnam — Travel Booking System

**System analysis & full development plan**
Prepared from three source artifacts: `TC.docx` (tour confirmation), `Tour_Agenda_VN19005_TOUR_AGENDA_.csv` (operational agenda), and `VN19005PNL.xlsx` (profit & loss). The worked example throughout is booking **VN19005**.

---

## 1. What the system does (one paragraph)

A quotation comes in from the agent (MakeMyTrip). The Booking Team turns it into a structured **Booking Reference** and a day-by-day **Tour Agenda**. The Ground Team verifies the operational reality (drivers, vehicles, tickets, supplier timings) and can bounce a booking back to Booking for correction. Accounts attaches the **P&L** that holds the real cost vs. the quoted price, and Accounts' confirmation of payment is the gate that lets the Ground Team actually buy tickets and pay suppliers. Travel Experience owns the customer relationship — reminders, collecting payment, and a final confirmation check 7 days out. Five days before departure the customer (or agent) gets a **Client Portal** showing trip status, payment status, and contacts. A Super Admin sits above all of it.

## 2. Source-document analysis — what each file becomes in the system

This is the most important section: the three files are not three systems, they are **three views of one booking**, captured at three stages by three different teams. The data model falls out of them directly.

### 2.1 `TC.docx` — Tour Confirmation → the **Booking** record

The Booking Team uploads this. It is the single source of truth for the booking header and is what generates the Booking Ref. Fields extracted:

| Field in document | System field | Example (VN19005) |
|---|---|---|
| Confirmation Number | `booking_ref` (primary key) | VN19005 |
| MMT - Booking ID | `agent_booking_id` | NL2202565846800 |
| Agent | `agent` | Make My Trip |
| File Handler | `file_handler` | ESTHER |
| Document version note | `version`, `amendment_note` | "02. AMENDED — Cruise Lunch Changed" |
| Arrival / Departure Date | `arrival_date` / `departure_date` | 18 May 2026 / 25 May 2026 |
| No. of Guests | `pax_adults` / `pax_children` | 2 adults / 0 |
| Total Tour Cost | `quoted_total` | USD 580.00 |
| Emergency contacts | `emergency_contacts[]` | Izzon, Helen, Tina, Senthoor |
| Guests Name block | **Passengers[]** | Vikas Arora (lead, 49), Richa Arora (46) |
| Flight Details | **Flights[]** | VJ1926, VJ517, VJ637, VJ1805 |
| Hotels table | **Accommodation[]** | 4 hotels across Hanoi, Sa Pa, Da Nang, HCMC |
| Itinerary Day 1–8 | **ItineraryItems[]** | 8 days of activities w/ inclusions/exclusions |
| Excludes / T&Cs / Special notes | `terms`, `exclusions`, `policy_notes` | cancellation 21-day rule, mandatory tips |

Critical detail: the document is already labelled **"02. AMENDED"** with a reason. **Versioning and amendment history is a hard requirement**, not a nice-to-have — bookings change after they are confirmed, and the system must track who changed what and when.

### 2.2 The Agenda CSV → the **Tour Agenda** (operational schedule)

Generated by the Booking Team from the confirmation, then operated on by the Ground Team and shown to the client. Each row is one service on one day:

| CSV column | System field |
|---|---|
| Date | `date` |
| Location | `location` (Hanoi, Sapa, Da Nang, Saigon) |
| From | `from_point` |
| To/Tours | `to_point` / activity name |
| Tour Details - Timewise | `details` |
| Meal Plan | `meal_plan` |
| Meeting Time | `meeting_time` |
| Service Type | `service_type` — enum: **PVT Transfer**, **SIC Transfer**, **Own Arrangement / Other vendor** |

The `service_type` enum matters operationally: PVT (private) and SIC (seat-in-coach/shared) need driver+vehicle assignment; "Own Arrangement" does not. Day 3 (20 May) in the example is an "Own Arrangement" day — the system must allow blank/own days.

### 2.3 `VN19005PNL.xlsx` → the **P&L** (financial record)

Uploaded and owned by Accounts. This is where the money lives, and the spreadsheet's formula reveals the whole financial model:

- **`MMT Rate`** column = the **revenue** side — what was sold to the agent. It sums to **580**, which equals the `Total Tour Cost` on the confirmation. This is the link that ties the P&L back to the quotation.
- **`Apple SIC Rate`, `PVT Rate PP`, `AD Entrance`, `CH entrnce`, `Other`** = the **actual supplier cost** components.
- **`Total APPLE Rate`** (per line) = computed cost for the whole group, via:
  `(SIC + PVT_PP + Other) × (adults + children) + (CH_entrance × children) + (AD_entrance × adults)`
- **`Num of AD` / `Num of CH`** = pax counts (2 / 0 for VN19005), stored once and referenced by every line.
- **Profit** = `Σ MMT Rate − Σ Total APPLE Rate` = `580 − 506.5` = **73.5**.

Each P&L line maps to an agenda activity, which maps to an itinerary item — the same activity appears in all three files (e.g. "Full-Day Shared Trip on 5-Star Ha Long Cruise"). The P&L **cost categories** the Accounts Team must be able to record and edit: hotel bills, tickets, guides, meals, day cruise, water, transport, tax/fees, flight tickets.

> **Design consequence:** store pax counts (adults/children) once on the Booking and reference them everywhere. The P&L line total is a *derived* value — compute it server-side from the rate components, never let it be typed in directly, exactly as the spreadsheet does with its formula.

## 3. Roles & responsibilities

Six roles (the brief says "4 user types" but lists six — treat it as six):

1. **Booking Team (`BT_USER`)** — uploads quotation, parses it into a Booking Ref, edits/corrects the parsed data, confirms the ref is correct, generates and edits the Tour Agenda. Handles correction requests sent back by Ground.
2. **Ground Team (`GT_USER`)** — reviews confirmed bookings; requests changes from BT if needed; once OK, assigns **driver + vehicle** and **adds/purchases tickets**; can view the P&L (from T−5); coordinates supplier timings with the client.
3. **Travel Experience (`TE_USER`)** — owns the customer relationship: contacts the client, sends reminders, processes/records payments, and **re-checks the booking is confirmed by T−7**.
4. **Accounts Team (`AC_USER`)** — views bookings; uploads & edits the P&L; **confirms payment per P&L line**, which unlocks Ground's purchasing.
5. **Client View (`CLIENT` — customer or agent)** — read-mostly portal, available from **T−5**: trip status, trip details, request updates, payment tab (pending / complete / rejected), contact traveller, contact driver.
6. **Super Admin (`SUPER_ADMIN`)** — full access; user management, role assignment, config, audit log, override.

## 4. Booking lifecycle (state machine)

The states below are the canonical status of a booking. (The diagram in the chat shows the same flow visually.)

| # | State | Owner | Entry condition / what happens | Exit → |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `DRAFT` | BT | Quotation uploaded, parsed, BT editing | BT confirms → 2 |
| 2 | `BT_CONFIRMED` | BT | Booking ref confirmed correct, agenda generated | Submitted to Ground → 3 |
| 3 | `GT_REVIEW` | GT | Ground inspecting operational detail | OK → 5 · changes needed → 4 |
| 4 | `CHANGE_REQUESTED` | BT | Ground returned it with notes; BT correcting | BT resubmits → 2 |
| 5 | `GT_VERIFIED` | GT | Ground approved; assigning driver/vehicle, drafting tickets | → 6 |
| 6 | `AWAITING_PAYMENT_CONFIRM` | AC | P&L uploaded; Accounts confirming line payments | Confirmed → 7 |
| 7 | `OPERATIONS_READY` | GT/TE | Tickets purchased, suppliers paid; TE running reminders/payments; re-check by T−7 | → 8 |
| 8 | `CLIENT_LIVE` | Client | T−5 reached; portal open to client/agent | trip starts → 9 |
| 9 | `IN_PROGRESS` | Ops | Trip underway | trip ends → 10 |
| 10 | `COMPLETED` | — | Trip finished, final P&L locked | archived |
| — | `CANCELLED` | any | Reachable from any pre-trip state | terminal |
| — | `AMENDED` | BT | Confirmation re-uploaded / changed post-confirmation; creates new version, re-enters review | → 3 |

**Key transition guards (business rules enforced by the system):**

- **G1 — Ground correction loop:** `GT_REVIEW → CHANGE_REQUESTED → BT_CONFIRMED → GT_REVIEW`. A booking can loop until Ground is satisfied. Every loop is logged.
- **G2 — Payment gate:** Ground may *draft* tickets in `GT_VERIFIED`, but cannot mark them **purchased/paid** until the matching P&L line is `payment_confirmed` by Accounts. This is the single most important rule in the system.
- **G3 — T−7 confirmation:** TE must complete a "booking confirmed" re-check at least 7 days before `arrival_date`. System flags bookings approaching T−7 that aren't re-checked.
- **G4 — T−5 client unlock:** Client Portal access and Ground's P&L visibility both open at T−5 (5 days before trip start). Before that the portal is locked.
- **G5 — Cancellation policy:** Per the T&Cs, 100% cancellation charge if cancelled <21 days before arrival. The system should compute and display the penalty window on the booking.

## 6. Data model (core entities)

```
User ──< (role) >── Role
Booking (booking_ref PK)
  ├──< Passenger        (name, type adult|child, age, is_lead, contact)
  ├──< Flight           (flight_no, date, from_apt, dep_time, to_apt, arr_time)
  ├──< Accommodation    (city, hotel, check_in, check_out, address, contact, nights, room_type, meal_type)
  ├──< ItineraryItem    (day_no, date, title, description, inclusions[], exclusions[])
  ├──  TourAgenda 1─1
  │       └──< AgendaItem (date, location, from, to, details, meal_plan, meeting_time, service_type)
  │                 └── 0..1 Assignment (driver, vehicle, contact)
  ├──< Ticket           (agenda_item_ref, type, qty, supplier, cost, status draft|purchased|paid)
  ├──  PNL 1─1
  │       ├── pax_adults, pax_children
  │       └──< PNLLineItem (activity, category, mmt_rate, sic_rate, pvt_pp, ad_entrance, ch_entrance, other,
  │                         total_cost DERIVED, payment_status pending|confirmed|rejected)
  ├──< Payment          (type, amount, method, status pending|complete|rejected, paid_at, reference)
  ├──< ChangeRequest    (raised_by, target_field/notes, status open|resolved, created_at, resolved_at)
  ├──< StatusEvent      (from_state, to_state, actor, timestamp)   ← state-machine audit
  └──< Version          (doc_snapshot, amendment_note, created_by, created_at)
```

Derived/computed values to enforce server-side: `PNLLineItem.total_cost` (the spreadsheet formula), `PNL.total_revenue = Σ mmt_rate`, `PNL.total_cost = Σ total_cost`, `PNL.profit = revenue − cost`, and `Booking.cancellation_deadline = arrival_date − 21 days`.

For the ER diagram, model these with crow's-foot notation in your tooling (e.g. dbdiagram.io / Mermaid `erDiagram`) — the relationships above translate directly.

## 7. Modules / features per role

### 7.1 Booking Team
- Quotation upload (`.docx`/`.pdf`) with a parser that pre-fills the booking form; BT reviews and corrects every field before confirming.
- Booking Ref generation (use the confirmation number as the ref, with uniqueness check).
- Tour Agenda builder (auto-draft from itinerary + flights, then editable grid matching the CSV columns).
- Confirm action that locks the header and submits to Ground.
- Change-request inbox: see Ground's notes, correct, resubmit.
- Amendment flow: re-upload a revised confirmation → new version + re-review.

### 7.2 Ground Team
- Review queue of `BT_CONFIRMED` bookings.
- Side-by-side compare of agenda vs. itinerary vs. flights.
- "Request change" → writes a `ChangeRequest`, moves booking to `CHANGE_REQUESTED`.
- Driver & vehicle assignment per PVT/SIC agenda item (driver directory + vehicle directory).
- Ticket management: draft tickets per activity; purchase only after Accounts confirms payment (G2).
- P&L view (read-only) from T−5.
- Client coordination prompts (e.g. confirm flight details ≥48h before, per T&Cs).

### 7.3 Travel Experience
- Client contact log + reminder scheduler (templated reminders).
- Payment processing/recording against the booking and P&L.
- T−7 confirmation checklist; system surfaces bookings nearing T−7 that aren't confirmed.

### 7.4 Accounts Team
- P&L upload (`.xlsx`) with parser mapping to `PNLLineItem`s, plus manual line editing.
- Categorised cost entry (hotel/tickets/guides/meals/cruise/water/transport/tax/flights).
- Per-line **payment confirm / reject** — the action that unlocks Ground purchasing.
- Profit dashboard (revenue, cost, margin) per booking and in aggregate.

### 7.5 Client / Agent Portal (T−5)
- Trip status banner + day-by-day itinerary (from the agenda, customer-facing fields only — **never** show cost/profit columns).
- "Request an update" form → raises a `ChangeRequest` routed to BT/GT.
- Payment tab: pending (from P&L), complete, rejected; payment methods/details.
- Contact cards: traveller (TE) and driver (from assignment).

### 7.6 Super Admin
- User CRUD, role assignment, system config (time-trigger windows, templates), full audit log, manual state override with reason.

## 8. Time triggers & notifications

| Trigger | Relative to | Fires to | Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Client portal unlock | T−5 (trip start) | Client, GT | Open portal; reveal P&L to GT |
| Re-check confirmation | T−7 (arrival) | TE | Task + reminder until done |
| Flight-detail deadline | T−48h (arrival) | GT, Client | "Airport transfer not guaranteed without flight details" |
| Cancellation window | T−21 (arrival) | BT, TE, Client | Show 100% penalty notice |
| Change requested | event | BT | New item in BT change inbox |
| Payment confirmed | event | GT | Ticket purchase unlocked |
| Payment rejected | event | TE, Client | Show in payment tab |

A lightweight scheduler (cron / job queue) evaluates date-based triggers daily; event-based triggers fire inline on the relevant transition.

## 9. Recommended technical architecture

A pragmatic, conventional stack — nothing exotic, easy to hire for:

- **Frontend:** React (or Next.js) SPA, role-aware routing, one app with role-gated views; a separate lighter surface for the Client Portal.
- **Backend:** REST (or GraphQL) API — Node/NestJS, Django, or Laravel all fit. Enforce RBAC and all state-machine guards **on the server**, never only in the UI.
- **Database:** PostgreSQL. Relational fits this domain (clear entities, financial integrity, joins across booking/agenda/P&L).
- **File parsing:** server-side workers for `.docx`/`.pdf` (e.g. `python-docx`, `pdfplumber`) and `.xlsx` (`openpyxl`). Keep parsing in a job queue — it's slow and shouldn't block the request.
- **Auth:** JWT/session + a roles/permissions table; the matrix in §5 is the seed data.
- **Jobs/scheduler:** a queue (BullMQ/Celery/Sidekiq) for parsing + time triggers + notifications.
- **Files:** object storage (S3-compatible) for uploaded quotations, P&L sheets, generated vouchers/agendas.
- **Audit:** append-only `StatusEvent` + `Version` tables; every state change and field edit recorded with actor + timestamp.

Money handling: store amounts in minor units (integers) with an explicit currency (USD here); compute P&L totals server-side from the rate components.

## 10. Key API surface (illustrative)

```
POST   /bookings/upload                 BT   → parse quotation, create DRAFT
GET    /bookings/:ref                    all (field-filtered by role)
PUT    /bookings/:ref                    BT   → edit header/passengers
POST   /bookings/:ref/confirm            BT   → DRAFT → BT_CONFIRMED
POST   /bookings/:ref/agenda             BT   → generate/edit agenda
POST   /bookings/:ref/submit-ground      BT   → BT_CONFIRMED (queue for GT)
POST   /bookings/:ref/change-request     GT   → GT_REVIEW → CHANGE_REQUESTED
POST   /bookings/:ref/resubmit           BT   → CHANGE_REQUESTED → BT_CONFIRMED
POST   /bookings/:ref/verify             GT   → GT_REVIEW → GT_VERIFIED
POST   /agenda-items/:id/assignment      GT   → set driver+vehicle
POST   /tickets                          GT   → draft ticket
POST   /tickets/:id/purchase             GT   → guarded by G2
POST   /bookings/:ref/pnl                AC   → upload/parse P&L
PUT    /pnl-lines/:id                     AC   → edit rate components (total recomputed)
POST   /pnl-lines/:id/confirm            AC   → payment_confirmed (unlocks G2)
POST   /payments                         TE   → record customer payment
POST   /bookings/:ref/recheck            TE   → T−7 confirmation
GET    /portal/:ref                       Client → trip status, payments, contacts (T−5+)
POST   /portal/:ref/request-update       Client → ChangeRequest
```

Every endpoint validates (a) the caller's role against §5 and (b) the current booking state against §4 before acting.

## 11. Phased development roadmap

**Phase 0 — Foundations (1–2 wks):** repo, auth, users/roles, RBAC table seeded from §5, the Booking entity + state machine skeleton, audit log. Nothing user-facing yet beyond login.

**Phase 1 — Booking core / MVP (3–4 wks):** quotation upload + parser → DRAFT, BT edit + confirm, agenda generation/editing, submit-to-Ground. Deliverable: a booking can go from uploaded `.docx` to `BT_CONFIRMED` with a clean agenda. *This is the demo-able MVP.*

**Phase 2 — Ground operations (2–3 wks):** Ground review queue, change-request loop (G1), driver/vehicle directories + assignment, draft tickets.

**Phase 3 — Accounts & the money (3 wks):** P&L upload + parser, line editing with server-side total formula, per-line payment confirm, the G2 purchase gate, profit dashboard.

**Phase 4 — Travel Experience (2 wks):** contact log, reminder scheduler, payment recording, T−7 re-check + the time-trigger engine.

**Phase 5 — Client Portal (2–3 wks):** T−5 unlock, trip status/details, payment tab, request-update, contact cards. Strict field-filtering so cost/profit never leak.

**Phase 6 — Admin, hardening, polish (2 wks):** Super Admin tools, notifications, versioning/amendments end-to-end, audit UI, security review, UAT.

Total rough order: ~3.5–4 months for a small team to a production-ready first version. Phases 1–3 are the spine; everything else hangs off them.

## 12. Decisions to confirm before building

1. **Booking Ref vs. confirmation number** — use the confirmation number (VN19005) directly as the ref, or generate a separate internal ID? (Recommend: confirmation number *is* the ref, with version suffixes for amendments.)
2. **Quotation format** — your brief says PDF but the file is `.docx`. Will both be supported? The parser differs.
3. **Currency** — P&L is in USD; will customer-facing payments be USD, INR, or VND? Multi-currency changes the payment module.
4. **"4 user types"** — confirmed as six roles (incl. Client and Super Admin)? The matrix assumes yes.
5. **Agent vs. customer in Client View** — same view or different (agents may see more)? Affects portal field-filtering.
6. **Who triggers cancellation** and how the 21-day penalty is recorded/charged.
7. **P&L line ↔ agenda activity linking** — auto-match by activity name, or manual mapping? Names differ slightly across the three files, so expect some manual reconciliation.
